These are merely notes taken from what Kamel told me. I'm still experimenting with this.

=== compiling (the C++) ===
You can get a x2, x3... x20 speed boost when compiling with -O2 or -O3, compared with nothing or -O0. You should definitely use -O2 unless you're specifically debugging ProBT itself.

=== profiling ===
Use the [=PL_TIME_SEC] macro (defined in plMath.h):
 double t;
 PL_TIME_SEC(
   _instantiated_question.draw(_values)
   , t);
 inform("draw: "<<t);

=== asking a question ===
There's an argument to allow to specify how to optimise the question, depending on how it's going to be used. I have a stored question that gets repeatedly instantiated and drawed upon, so I use:
 jd.ask(question, 
   _motors, 
   _sensors[i] ^ _phid ^ _vd ^ _phi_prev ^ _v_prev,
   PL_OPTIMIZE_UPDATE_TIME);

=== to compile or not to compile the instantiated question before drawing ===
You should try not compiling it. If the engine can go Gibbs when drawing, you'll be a winner. If not, it will warn you about doing a Metropolis sampling, and then, I'm not sure what to do yet.
