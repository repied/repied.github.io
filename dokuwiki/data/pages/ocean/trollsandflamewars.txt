I list here links to trolls and flamewars in the free software community, which I enjoy a lot.

= MPlayer =
Mmh, [MPlayer http://mplayerhq.hu]. First quality flamewars, 24/7/365. Two of the most active project members (A'rpi, the founder, and Gabucino, doc writer, website maintainer and PR) having just left may or may not have a connection with the friendliness of this working environment.
* The [oh so cool licensing flamewar http://mplayerhq.hu/pipermail/mplayer-g2-dev/2004-February/000789.html] (initiated by a post by A'rpi, who proposed a dual licensing scheme: GPL/commercial). Includes such arguments as:
 If there's going to be ANY proprietary licensing of mplayer code, I'm off the project, forever.
 (D Richard Felker III)
* [Handling of the KISS case http://mplayerhq.hu/pipermail/mplayer-dev-eng/2004-March/024433.html] (that company which seems to have stolen GPL MPlayer code): developers fight with each other -- includes license flames, FSF & RMS bashing, and very sweet personal attacks.
* [A guy being kicked off the development mailing list because his signature does not respect netiquette: http://mplayerhq.hu/pipermail/mplayer-dev-eng/2004-April/025494.html]. The signature in question:

* ["When in doubt, flame" http://mplayerhq.hu/pipermail/mplayer-dev-eng/2004-March/024634.html]: a guy leaves, says thank-you-working-with-you-was-nice, and gets a nice "good riddance, that will teach you" /au revoir/.
* [Your being not as competent as me allows me to treat you like a complete idiot http://mplayerhq.hu/pipermail/mplayer-g2-dev/2004-April/000898.html]. Flaming an innocent must feel so good, when you have no risk to be contradicted by anyone.
